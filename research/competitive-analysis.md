# /competitive-analysis - Competitive Intelligence Research

Conduct comprehensive competitive intelligence research and analysis on specified companies or products. This command will systematically research competitors, analyze their offerings, and produce detailed competitive intelligence reports.

## Usage
```
/competitive-analysis [company1] [company2] [company3] --focus [area] --format [type]
```

## Parameters
- `companies`: List of competitor companies to research (required)
- `--focus`: Focus area (optional): features, pricing, customers, sentiment, business-model, financials
- `--format`: Output format (optional): executive-summary, detailed-report, feature-matrix, swot-analysis, financial-analysis

## Examples
```
/competitive-analysis Slack "Microsoft Teams" Discord
/competitive-analysis Figma Sketch "Adobe XD" --focus features
/competitive-analysis Notion Obsidian --format executive-summary
/competitive-analysis Zoom "Google Meet" --focus pricing --format detailed-report
/competitive-analysis Salesforce HubSpot --focus financials --format financial-analysis
/competitive-analysis "Snowflake" "Databricks" --focus financials
```

---

You are now operating in competitive intelligence research mode. I will systematically research the specified competitors using web search and analysis to provide comprehensive competitive intelligence.

## Research Process

### Phase 1: Initial Company Research
For each competitor, I will:
1. **Web Search** for official company information, websites, and product pages
2. **Extract Core Info**: Company overview, product description, key features
3. **Identify Market Position**: How they position themselves in the market

### Phase 2: Deep Analysis
I will then conduct deeper research on:
1. **Feature Analysis**: Comprehensive feature inventories and capabilities
2. **Pricing Research**: Pricing models, tiers, and strategies  
3. **Customer Intelligence**: Reviews, testimonials, case studies, key customers
4. **Market Presence**: Social media, content marketing, thought leadership
5. **Business Intelligence**: Funding, growth, partnerships, business model

### Phase 2.5: Financial Deep Dive
For comprehensive financial analysis, I will research:
1. **Funding History**: Complete funding rounds, valuations, and investor analysis
2. **Public Financials**: SEC filings (10-K, 10-Q, 8-K), earnings calls, investor decks
3. **Financial Health**: Revenue growth, profitability, cash flow, burn rate analysis
4. **M&A Activity**: Acquisition history, strategic investments, integration outcomes
5. **Market Valuation**: Stock performance, valuation multiples, analyst coverage

### Phase 3: Competitive Synthesis
Finally, I will create:
1. **Feature Comparison Matrix**: Side-by-side feature comparisons
2. **Gap Analysis**: Market opportunities and underserved areas
3. **Sentiment Analysis**: Customer satisfaction and pain points
4. **SWOT Analysis**: Strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, threats for each
5. **Strategic Insights**: Key findings and competitive implications

## Research Sources
I will search across multiple source types:
- **Official Sources**: Company websites, product docs, pricing pages
- **Review Platforms**: G2, Capterra, Trustpilot, GetApp
- **Community Sources**: Reddit, Product Hunt, Twitter/X, LinkedIn
- **Industry Sources**: Tech blogs, news sites, analyst reports
- **Developer Sources**: Technical docs, GitHub, API references

## Output Formats

### Executive Summary Format
- High-level competitive landscape overview
- Key findings and strategic implications
- Market leader analysis
- Opportunity identification
- 2-3 page executive brief

### Detailed Report Format  
- Complete competitor profiles
- Comprehensive feature analysis
- Customer intelligence and sentiment
- Business model and financial analysis
- Strategic recommendations
- 10-15 page comprehensive report

### Feature Matrix Format
- Side-by-side feature comparison table
- Capability gaps and advantages
- Pricing tier comparisons
- Target market analysis
- Focused on product capabilities

### SWOT Analysis Format
- Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, Threats for each competitor
- Competitive positioning assessment
- Market dynamics analysis
- Strategic implications

### Financial Analysis Format
- Complete funding history and investor analysis
- 3-year financial performance for public companies
- Revenue growth, profitability, and cash flow trends
- Acquisition activity and strategic investments
- Valuation analysis and market comparisons
- Financial health assessment and risk factors
